ROCK BOTTOM

Roy had always been an introverted person who preferred the peace and quiet of his own company over the noise and chaos of society. That's what he called it "chaos". Despite living in Welcome City, a bustling metropolis which almost everything is in harmony, Roy felt increasingly out of place and overwhelmed by the constant sensory stimulation that surrounded him.

One day, after a particularly stressful week at work, Roy decided that he had had enough of metropolis life. He longed for an escape from it all, a place where he could live a quiet, solitary existence away from the noise and constant stimulation of the city. He always dreamed of a mountaintop small cabinet with essential stuff and nothing more. So he decided to spend almost all of his savings on a place where he could live the rest of his life in isolation.

Despite having the means to buy a mountaintop retreat, Roy eventually chose a lake bottom as his location of choice. He figured that being isolated beneath the water's surface would be the ultimate form of solitude. He built his bunker deep beneath a lake far away from city, designed so that once inside, there was no way out. He removed all communication devices and relied solely on solar panels for electricity and resources.

Initially, Roy found solace in taking care of the plants and reading books, but over time, he became consumed by loneliness and depression. Despite his initial desires to escape society, he found himself longing for human interaction once again. He realized that he had spent all his savings to escape the very thing he now craved.

Determined to find a way out, Roy started to dig a tunnel under the lake relentlessly. He was desperate for any kind of human connection, even though he had once paid to avoid it. Six months into his escape, the tunnel was almost complete, and finally, he saw a light in the distance. Just as he thought he missed sunlight, it turned out to be just a street light. He realized that Welcome City had expanded and now surrounded the lake in just six months.

As he pondered his situation, he saw a woman waving at him from a distance. To his surprise, he realized it was his ex-girlfriend. They hugged each other, and for the first time in a year, he felt the warmth of human touch. He learned that his ex-girlfriend had just moved somewhere nearby with her husband and was expecting her second child. Although the news saddened him, he was grateful for the human connection. After they said goodbye, he realized that hugging actually made him feel much better. Thinking about the hug, he realized all he wants is to hug his own mother once again, but it would take a lot of money to travel to the other side of Welcome City. So he came up with an idea to use his remaining funds. He bought a holographic sign and made it display "HUG ME 5$," and people began lining up to hug him.

As the line grew longer and Roy grew tired of hugging, he decided he had enough money to travel to the other side of Welcome City. There was still a long line in front of him, so he came up with an idea. He told the people in line to hug each other, and they complied, bringing happiness and a sense of relief to all.

Once he arrived home, he could hear the news from inside about today's hug queue playing on TV. Stepping inside, he saw his mother crying. He immediately ran up to his mom and hugged her tightly in tears, feeling a sense of love and warmth that he had missed for so long. Despite hugging hundreds of people that day, nothing compared to the feeling of hugging his own mother.
